TOOLS

Dive mask

A dive mask is the goggles that the divers use to be able to see underwater. It is mainly used in order to prevent unclear or blurry vision underwater as well as to be able to do equalization, which needs to be done in order to avoid nose pain.

A dive mask usually has different size, color, material, and shapes, aiming for an ideal mask for each person with different face characteristics.

Snorkel

Snorkel is the equipment that allows people to breathe as there’s no oxygen to be inhaled or taken. Since divers are unable to breathe through their nose, they make use of a snorkel instead to breathe via their mouth.

BCD

BCD is a buoyancy control device. It is the device that could assist the diver to control their buoyancy underwater through deflating and inflating it.

BCD could also help the diver to keep the distance between the coral reef and themselves as the equipment capable of setting up positive and neutral buoyancy of the diver.

Regulator

Regulator is the breathing tube that provides the air to the divers to breathe easily underwater. Regulator has the ability to keep the decomposed air that comes from the cylinder or tank.

Regulator also divided into two parts, which is the first stage regulator and the second stage regulator. The second stage regulator is used when the diver’s buddy runs out of oxygen. This is mainly sharing the air that is being decomposed in the same tank or cylinder.

Cylinder

A cylinder is mainly the container that the divers usually use to fill the decomposed air, which is the air that they will utilize as they get into the water.

This will be the equipment that usually can be seen positioned on the divers back, wearing it like a bag.

Weight System

Weight system is the equipment that has its own role to help to maintain the buoyancy of the divers, which is the alternative of BCD, using it like a belt.

Fins

Fins are the equipment that will be put on last due to its location, which is on our feets.

Fins are used by the divers to have a lighter and easy movement in the water, which is due to its long and large surface area characteristics. This is beneficial for the divers when they need to go against the water current.

SPG

SPG is the equipment or the device that helps the divers to determine the amount of air in the cylinder or tank (in bar (metric), or psi(imperial, pounds per square inch)).

SPG is also the equipment that helps to lead or direct the divers to a certain area along with the certain depth of the divers’ location.

Prepare the scuba tank or cylinder, BCD, and regulator set

02

Examine your scuba tank or cylinder

03

Pay attention to the scuba cylinder’s o-ring condition

04

Over the tank or cylinder valve, slide the BCD

05

Tying up the straps on your tank or cylinder

06

Tighten the straps as tight as you can to keep them from coming free

07

Ensuring the head clearance (the height of the scuba tank valve), pay attention to the rear of the bcd's neck align

08

Take the regulator set's dust cap off

09

Ensuring that we place the regulator mouthpieces on our right side of the BCD

10

Connect the scuba cylinder's yoke valve to the regulator set

11

The yolk screw of the cylinder should be tightened

12

The low pressure inflator should be connected to the BCD

13

Ensure that a click sound is produced

14

Position the second stage regulator between our chin and the ribcage corner

15

Open the scuba cylinder's valve carefully to check the tank pressure

16

After you hear the device pressured, fully open the tank valve and turn it back a quarter turn.

17

Use spg to check the scuba tank pressure

18

Try to inflate and deflate your BCD

19

Regulator's clearance

20

Eliminating water from the mask
